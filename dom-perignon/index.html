DOM Perignon

A Chrome extension named after the innovator Dom Pierre Pérignon.

  • The DOM (Document Object Model) is the underlying structure of every web page's elements.
  • DOM Perignon uses that for eight different perspectives.
  • Live previews below against a sample 'Kestral Fabrications' page.
v1.2.1
The collection

01 Ants

A colony of crawling SVG ants with realistic leg gait. Bonus chaos on sites with empty white space.

Most popular

02 Champagne Bubbles

Pale bubbles rise with realistic fizz physics. Click to pop. Corks ricochet across every twenty-eight seconds or so.

House signature

03 Logo Bounce House

The site's logo, loose and bouncing - eight small copies. Sharper rendering on low-res favicons.

04 You Got This

A little laid-back inspiration. Sixty affirmations in desert/autumn hues - saffron, terracotta, sienna, mustard, sage, rosewood, adobe clay.

Preview unavailable

05 Gravity Well

Page elements slowly fall and pile at the bottom. Click any falling piece to rescue it.

06 Aquarium

The page becomes the back wall of a tank. Kelp, bubbles, fish, ripples.

07 Watchers

Eyes appear quietly across the page. They follow your cursor. They do not blink.

08 DVD Bouncer

If you're feeling nostalgic and uninspired.

A self-contained Chrome extension. ~44 KB. Manifest V3. No network calls, no telemetry - it only paints over the pages you open.

Load as an unpacked extension

  1. Unzip the file anywhere stable on your machine (e.g. Documents/dom-perignon).
  2. Open Chrome and visit chrome://extensions.
  3. Toggle Developer mode on (top-right corner of the page).
  4. Click Load unpacked and select the unzipped folder.
  5. Pin the toolbar icon (the puzzle-piece menu, then the pin next to "DOM Perignon"). Click it, flip Active on, pick a hijink.
Never installed an unpacked extension before? Chrome's official walkthrough is here: developer.chrome.com - Load unpacked.
