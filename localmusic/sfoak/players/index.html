10 attempts · 1 problem

Make the music keep playing — even when you look away.

A cohesive desktop Spotify player for SF + Oakland live shows has one stubborn bug: when you switch tabs or minimize, the current song finishes and playback stops dead instead of rolling into the next track. Below are ten escalating fixes, each bolder than the last. The arc is a single idea getting more committed: stop advancing tracks in the browser — and where the browser still gets in the way, hand off to a device that isn't a browser tab.

🎧
To hear full tracks: attempts 3–10 use the Spotify Web Playback SDK and need Spotify Premium — connect once (top right) and it works across all of them. Attempts 1–2 use the embed and play full tracks if you're already signed into Spotify in this browser. No Premium? You still get 30-second previews.
⚠️
The honest part (no overclaiming): a browser tab is a suspendable device. The current song keeps playing in the background, but at the silent gap between songs Chrome can suspend the tab — and a suspended tab can't start the next stream, no matter whose “queue” it is. So 5–7 & 9 are the best a pure in-tab player can do, and must be verified on a real machine with a speaker meter (headless can't reproduce the freeze). The only guaranteed background is attempt 8: hand playback to your phone or the Spotify desktop app — they aren't tabs, so they advance natively while this page is just the remote.

The lesson, in one line.

You can't make a hidden browser tab reliably advance its own music — a tab is a device the OS is allowed to suspend. The win isn't a cleverer keep-alive; it's refusing to depend on the tab at all: hand the music to Spotify's servers, and for a real guarantee, to a device that was never a tab.
